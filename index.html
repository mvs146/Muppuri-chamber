About the Chamber

A distinguished leader of the Thogata Veera Kshatriya community, Muppuri Venkata Suresh has dedicated his life to the advancement, unity, and prosperity of his people across Andhra Pradesh and beyond.

The Thogata Veera Kshatriyas claim noble descent from both the Surya Vamsam (Solar Dynasty) and Chandra Vamsam (Lunar Dynasty). The presiding goddess Chowdeswari Devi is worshipped across five states, with Nandavaram as the community's holiest pilgrimage site.

The Muppuri Chamber is a sovereign royal institution — a gathering place for commerce, culture, advocacy, and generational leadership rooted in the warrior spirit of the Thogata Veera Kshatriya lineage.

A Royal Legacy Spanning Millennia

History of Thogata Veera Kshatriya

From ancient warrior kings of the Lunar and Solar dynasties to the proud guardians of Andhra heritage — a lineage stretching across four thousand years.

Lunar and Solar Dynasty Origins

The Thogata lineage traces to the Chandra Vamsam and Surya Vamsam — the twin royal bloodlines of ancient India. Their 108 sacred gotras originate from the seven primordial Saptarishi sages. The great Ikshwaku dynasty connects the Thogata to the same bloodline as Lord Rama.

The Chola Confederacy and Warrior Alliances

Historical inscriptions link the Thogata Veera Kshatriyas to the Early Chola Confederacy as trusted military allies. Rock inscriptions at Amaravati and Nagarjunakonda record their service to the Satavahana dynasty, establishing them as a recognised warrior aristocracy of the Deccan.

The Kakatiya Dynasty — Gangeya Vamsam

The Gangeya clan rose to rule as the great Kakatiya dynasty from Warangal. Queen Rudrama Devi (r. 1262-1289 CE), celebrated by Marco Polo, commanded vast Thogata forces. The Ramappa Temple (UNESCO World Heritage 2021) stands as eternal testament to their genius.

Feudal Lords and the Battle of Bobbili

Thogata Veera Kshatriya chieftains established independent principalities across Andhra. The legendary Battle of Bobbili (1757 CE) — in which Thogata warriors chose death over surrender — is immortalised in Telugu ballads as the eternal epitome of Kshatriya honour.

Five States, One Sovereign Community

Today the Thogata Veera Kshatriya community thrives across Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu. The Muppuri Chamber stands as the modern sovereign institution to advance this eternal royal legacy across the world.
